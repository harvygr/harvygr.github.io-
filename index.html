Khorvaire

Introduction

The continent of Khorvaire is part of the fantasy world, Eberron, built to run the tabletop role-playing game, Dungeons & Dragons. It is a varied place filled with intrigue, adventure, and challenge. It has long been occupied by warring nations, but two years ago, all involved parties found a hardwon peace in the midst of tragedy. The question remains if (or when) the nations will go to war once more.

History

The Creation of Eberron

The story of the Progenitor Dragons is widely known and believed across Eberron as how the world came to be. The story holds that three cosmic beings, the dragons Siberys, Eberron, and Khyber, created thirteen planes of being, each embodying a specific concept. All of these concepts were meant to manifest in the Material Plane. It would be a realm of contradiction: war and peace, order and chaos. However, Khyber wanted total power of this new reality. In seeking this, she ambushed and destroyed Siberys. When Eberron discovered this, she fought Khyber, holding her in her coils, but she would not die. So, Eberron imprisoned her in her claws forever, a living prison for a traitorous prisoner. These events explain the ring of golden dragonshards surrounding the planet as the remains of Siberys, the world and source of life as Eberron, and the underdark and origin of fiends and demons as Khyber.

The Last War

Over 100 years ago, the nations of Aundair, Breland, Cyre, Karrnath, and Thrane wered united under the kingdom of Galifar. This era of harmony ended with the death of King Jarot. When the King passed, he left no heir, and the nations decended into chaos as they tried to claim the throne for themselves. The war for the throne lasted a century and grew to involve not just the nations of Galifar, but the entire continent. The war came to a startling end with the Mourning, an arcane force that destroyed the whole nation of Cyre, now dubbed the Mournland. Out of fear, involved parties gathered at the island of Thronehold to construct the aptly-named Treaty of Thronehold. However, it can be assured that if the cause of the Mourning is discovered and proven controllable, the war will begin anew. For now though, the people of Khorvaire are left to pick up the pieces of a war never won.
